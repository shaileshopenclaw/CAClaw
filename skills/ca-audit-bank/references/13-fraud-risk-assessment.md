## Bank Audit | SA 240 | ICAI GN 2025 | RBI Fraud Monitoring Framework

## PART 1: AUDITOR'S RESPONSIBILITY FOR FRAUD (SA 240)

```
SA 240 — THE AUDITOR'S RESPONSIBILITIES RELATING TO FRAUD:

PRIMARY RESPONSIBILITY: MANAGEMENT + THOSE CHARGED WITH GOVERNANCE
  → Design and implement controls to prevent and detect fraud
  → Board/Audit Committee: oversight of management's processes

AUDITOR'S RESPONSIBILITY:
  → Obtain REASONABLE ASSURANCE that financial statements are
    free from MATERIAL MISSTATEMENT due to fraud OR error
  → Maintain professional SKEPTICISM throughout the audit
  → NOT responsible for detecting ALL fraud (inherent limitations)
  → But MUST respond to fraud risks identified

AUDITOR'S ACTIONS WHEN FRAUD DISCOVERED:
  1. Assess nature and extent of fraud
  2. Evaluate impact on financial statements
  3. Report to appropriate level of management (not the fraudster!)
  4. If senior management involved → report to Board/ACB
  5. Consider whether audit report needs modification
  6. Consider legal/regulatory reporting obligations
  7. For banks: Fraud must be mentioned in audit report if material
```

## PART 4: FRAUD INDICATORS — COMPREHENSIVE

### ADVANCE FRAUD INDICATORS

```
DOCUMENTATION FRAUD:
❌ Title deed: not original, photocopies only, suspicious stamps
❌ Property registered in name different from borrower
❌ Same property mortgaged to multiple banks (search reveals)
❌ Fabricated audited financials (inconsistencies vs tax return)
❌ Inflated income certificates (KYC mismatch with ITR)
❌ Stock statements: company-stamped but without proper letterhead
❌ Fake purchase invoices (goods never received)
❌ Board resolutions with non-existent directors
❌ Valuation reports: inflated by related/connected valuers
❌ CIBIL match: borrower name ≠ CIBIL registered name

OPERATIONAL FRAUD IN ADVANCES:
❌ Loan disbursed to account different from borrower's account
❌ Loan proceeds used to repay another loan of same borrower (round-trip)
❌ Cash withdrawal of full loan immediately after disbursement
❌ Multiple CC accounts at different branches for same business
❌ CC/OD balance consistently at maximum limit (no operational pattern)
❌ Credits in CC account ONLY at end of month (to avoid SMA/NPA)
❌ CC account: very high debit transactions vs low actual sales
❌ Related party transactions: supplier-buyer both funded by bank
❌ Staff guaranteeing their relatives' loans
❌ CRILC shows borrower as SMA at another bank → bank not informed
❌ IBC admission: borrower filed without bank's knowledge
❌ Devolved LCs not converted to loans (hidden off-balance sheet)

EVERGREENING (MOST COMMON):
❌ Fresh loan disbursed 1-2 days before another loan's due date
❌ Credits in overdraft from unrelated entities (sister/associated firms)
❌ Loan disbursed to related party who immediately deposits in borrower's account
❌ Credit entry matches exactly with overdue interest (interest ever-greening)
❌ Restructuring done without genuine business improvement evidence
❌ Modification of repayment schedule just before NPA date
❌ Disbursement to account with low KYC details
```

### DEPOSIT FRAUD INDICATORS

```
❌ Fictitious FD: receipt issued, no corresponding credit in books
❌ Duplicate FD: same FD number issued to two customers
❌ Premature FD payout to wrong account or without customer request
❌ Dormant account activated without customer interaction
❌ Cash withdrawal from dormant account with forged signature
❌ NEFT/RTGS from dormant accounts to unfamiliar beneficiaries
❌ Changed mobile number on account without customer verification
❌ Multiple accounts for same PAN with inconsistent KYC
❌ Bulk FD with amounts just below DEAF threshold (gaming)
❌ Large cash deposits from multiple unrelated parties (structuring/smurfing)
```

### TECHNOLOGY / CBS FRAUD

```
❌ Backdated CBS entries without override log
❌ NPA flag reversed in CBS without payment receipt
❌ Interest on NPA accounts accrued in CBS (should be suspended)
❌ System-generated interest rate differs from account master rate
❌ Employee accessing accounts of VIP/unrelated customers
❌ Same IP address used for multiple fraudulent transactions
❌ Transaction amount just below reporting threshold (₹10L CTR threshold)
❌ SWIFT messages modified without authorisation (SWIFT fraud — rare)
❌ Vendor/maintenance window: transactions during downtime not logged
❌ Mirror accounts: duplicate accounts with slight name variation
```

## PART 5: WILFUL DEFAULTER IDENTIFICATION

```
WILFUL DEFAULT — DEFINITION (RBI Master Circular):
A wilful default occurs when:

1. Default despite adequate cash flow / ability to repay (deliberate non-payment)
2. Funds diverted for purposes other than sanctioned purpose
3. Funds siphoned off (not available in business)
4. Assets disposed off without bank's knowledge/consent
5. Intentional misrepresentation to obtain funds

PROCESS FOR WILFUL DEFAULTER DECLARATION:
Step 1: Evidence of wilful default gathered
Step 2: Show Cause Notice to borrower / guarantor
Step 3: Personal hearing given (principle of natural justice)
Step 4: Independent review committee of senior bank officials
Step 5: Board-level committee approval for declaration
Step 6: CRILC reporting of wilful defaulter
Step 7: Credit bureau (CIBIL, Equifax etc.) reporting
Step 8: RBI website publication (for large defaults)

CONSEQUENCES OF WILFUL DEFAULTER DECLARATION:
→ No new credit facilities from any bank
→ No restructuring of existing credit
→ Director disqualified from joining any bank's board
→ Criminal proceedings under IPC (fraud)

AUDITOR'S CHECKS:
[ ] Bank has a Board-approved Wilful Defaulter Policy?
[ ] Procedure followed for each declared wilful defaulter?
[ ] CRILC reporting done within 7 days of declaration?
[ ] Credit bureau reporting done?
[ ] Fresh credit not extended to declared wilful defaulters?
[ ] Wilful Defaulter Certificate: issued by auditor with full list
```

## PART 6: FRAUD AUDIT PROCEDURES (SA 240)

```
FRAUD RISK ASSESSMENT PROCEDURES:
[ ] Inquiries of management:
    - How does management identify fraud risks?
    - Have any frauds occurred? What was the response?
    - Is there pressure to meet performance targets?
[ ] Inquiries of internal audit:
    - Fraud indicators observed during internal audit?
    - Pending fraud investigations?
[ ] Analytical procedures:
    - Unusual journal entries (large round numbers, year-end)
    - Unexpected changes in ratios
    - Inconsistencies in NPA movement
[ ] Journal entry testing (CBS log):
    - Entries made directly to suspense/miscellaneous GL
    - Entries made after hours or on holidays
    - Entries made by IT/system admin users
    - Entries reversed shortly after posting

RESPONSE TO FRAUD RISK:
1. Overall response: assign more experienced staff; increase unpredictability
2. Specific responses:
   Revenue recognition: test appropriateness of accounting policies
   Management override: test journal entries; review estimates
   Inventory/assets: surprise physical counts
3. Increased sample sizes in high-risk areas
4. Surprise visits to branches with high fraud risk indicators
```

## PART 7: FRAUD REPORTING IN AUDIT REPORT

```
WHEN TO REPORT FRAUD IN AUDIT REPORT:

MATERIAL FRAUD → MUST disclose in audit report:
"During the course of our audit, we noticed/were informed of a fraud
amounting to ₹___ Lakhs involving [brief description] at [branch name].
The fraud was detected on [date] and Fraud Monitoring Return (FMR) was
filed with RBI on [date]. Provisions of ₹___ Lakhs have been made."

AUDIT REPORT MODIFICATION:
→ Fraud amount > materiality and provision not made:
  QUALIFIED OPINION (provision shortfall)
→ Fraud creating going concern doubt:
  EMPHASIS OF MATTER or GOING CONCERN QUALIFICATION

CARO 2020 (Para 3(x)) — MANDATORY REPORTING:
"We report/have nothing to report that during the year:
(a) Any fraud BY the bank was noticed or reported
(b) Any fraud ON the bank was noticed or reported
[If fraud exists: details of amount, nature, status]"
```
