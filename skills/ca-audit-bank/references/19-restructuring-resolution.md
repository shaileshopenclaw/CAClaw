## PART 1: RBI RESOLUTION FRAMEWORK (June 2019 — KV Kamath Committee)

### Background
RBI issued the "Prudential Framework for Resolution of Stressed Assets" circular on June 7, 2019 replacing the older S4A, SDR, CDR and 5/25 schemes.

```
KEY PRINCIPLES:
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
→ Resolution to be driven by lenders, not RBI
→ Flexibility to design resolution plan
→ Backed by binding Inter-Creditor Agreement (ICA)
→ 30-day review period from date of default
→ 180-day resolution period
→ Additional provisioning for delayed resolution

REVIEW PERIOD (30 DAYS):
  From date when borrower first defaults → lenders must decide:
  Option A: Initiate Resolution Plan
  Option B: File for Insolvency (IBC)
  Option C: Other recovery actions

RESOLUTION PERIOD (180 DAYS FROM ICA SIGNING):
  Resolution Plan (RP) must be implemented within 180 days
  If not implemented: additional provision kicks in

PROVISION ESCALATION (post-June 2019 framework):
  Day 0 (default): Standard IRACP provisions apply
  + 180 days: No RP → additional 20% provision
  + 365 days (= 180 + 180): No RP → further additional 15% provision
  Total additional provision = 35% (on top of existing IRACP provisions)
  If RP implemented within 180 days: 5% additional (standard restructured rate)
```

### ICA (Inter-Creditor Agreement)

```
ICA REQUIREMENTS:
[ ] All lenders (banks + NBFCs) must sign ICA
[ ] Decision binding if: approved by ≥75% of lenders by VALUE
    AND: ≥60% of lenders by NUMBER
[ ] Minority lenders: bound by majority decision
[ ] IBA model ICA: should be used (not customised individually)

AUDITOR'S CHECKS FOR ICA ACCOUNTS:
[ ] ICA signed by all lenders?
[ ] Aggregate exposure of lenders: all included?
[ ] Lead bank identified?
[ ] Timeline for resolution plan: 180 days from ICA date?
[ ] Monitoring mechanism: designated as per ICA?
[ ] Majority decision compliance: bank voted? decision implemented?
```

## PART 4: IBC/INSOLVENCY AND BANKRUPTCY CODE AUDIT

```
IBC 2016 — COMPLETE FLOW:

STEP 1: TRIGGER
  Corporate Debtor defaults on debt ≥₹1 Crore (as revised 2020)
  Application can be filed by:
    → Financial Creditor (bank): Section 7
    → Operational Creditor: Section 9
    → Corporate Debtor itself: Section 10

STEP 2: NCLT ADMISSION (Section 14 Moratorium)
  NCLT admits application → CIRP (Corporate Insolvency Resolution Process) begins
  Moratorium: no suits, recovery proceedings, asset transfers
  IRP (Interim Resolution Professional) appointed within 14 days
  
STEP 3: COMMITTEE OF CREDITORS (CoC)
  All financial creditors → form CoC
  Bank's voting share = Bank's admitted claim / Total admitted claims × 100
  Major decisions require ≥66% CoC vote

STEP 4: RESOLUTION PLAN SUBMISSION
  Information memorandum (IM) prepared by RP
  Resolution applicants submit plans
  CoC evaluates: maximum value, going concern, employee protection
  Approved plan: ≥66% CoC vote → NCLT approval

STEP 5: NCLT APPROVAL OF PLAN
  NCLT approves → implementation within 90 days
  Bank's haircut = Admitted Claim − Amount per approved plan
  Haircut accounting: write off against provisions first, then P&L

STEP 6: LIQUIDATION (If no plan approved in time)
  CoC votes for liquidation OR CIRP period expires without plan
  Liquidator appointed
  Asset sale proceeds: Priority waterfall (Section 53):
    1. CIRP and liquidation costs
    2. Workmen dues (24 months)
    3. Secured creditors (to extent of security)
    4. Employee dues
    5. Government dues
    6. Remaining secured creditor claims
    7. Unsecured creditors (including unsecured bank loans)
    8. Preference shareholders
    9. Equity shareholders

PROVISION TABLE (AUDITOR'S REFERENCE):
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
STAGE                               | MINIMUM PROVISION
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
Pre-CIRP admission (sub-std/D1 etc) | Per IRACP norms
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
CIRP Admitted (RBI List 1 — first   | 50% immediately OR
12 large accounts — already done)   | IRACP norm, whichever higher
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
CIRP Admitted (subsequent accounts) | 50% (within 6 months of CIRP)
                                    | 100% if CIRP >6 months old
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
Resolution Plan Approved by NCLT    | Bank's haircut portion:
                                    | 100% provision on haircut
                                    | Remaining amount per plan:
                                    | Downgrade provision if NPA
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
```

## PART 5: SARFAESI PROCEEDINGS AUDIT

```
SARFAESI ACT 2002 — STEP-BY-STEP:

SECTION 13(2): 60-DAY NOTICE
  → Issued after account classified as NPA
  → Notice to borrower: pay within 60 days or bank takes possession
  → Service: registered post + personal delivery + newspaper (if needed)
  [ ] Verify notice date, amount demanded, service acknowledgement

SECTION 13(4): POSSESSION NOTICE
  → After 60 days (if borrower hasn't paid)
  → Bank serves possession notice
  → Takes possession: physical or symbolic
  [ ] Verify possession date, inventory of assets taken

VALUATION BEFORE SALE:
  → Empanelled valuer must value asset post-possession
  → Reserve price = NOT LESS than forced sale value
  [ ] Verify valuation report: age (< 6 months before auction), empanelled valuer

SECTION 13(8): SALE NOTICE
  → 30 days before auction date
  → Published in 2 newspapers (1 regional language)
  → Borrower can still pay and stop sale
  [ ] Verify publication dates, content of notice, reserve price disclosed

AUCTION / SALE:
  → E-auction through MSTC/eBKray/other approved portal
  → Highest bid ≥ reserve price: sale valid
  → Registration within 3 months of sale
  [ ] Verify auction records, winner, amount, registration status

RECOVERY AND ACCOUNTING:
  → Sale proceeds: applied in order: costs → overdue interest → principal
  → Surplus: returned to borrower (Section 13(9))
  → Provision reversal: proportionate to recovery
  → If sale below outstanding: loss recognised; excess provision released

DM/CMM ORDER (Section 14):
  → For physical possession: bank can approach District Magistrate
  → DM must give order within 30 days (60 days if requires)
  [ ] DM/CMM order obtained? Date? Status of physical possession?
```

## PART 6: OTS / COMPROMISE SETTLEMENT AUDIT

```
ONE-TIME SETTLEMENT (OTS) — AUDIT FRAMEWORK:

RBI CIRCULAR ON COMPROMISE SETTLEMENTS (2023):
  RBI/2023-24/07 DOR.STR.REC.20/21.04.048/2023-24 (June 8, 2023)
  Allows compromise settlements for NPAs/stressed accounts
  NO RBI PRIOR APPROVAL required (bank's Board/committee decides)

ELIGIBILITY:
  → All types of NPAs (sub-standard, doubtful, loss)
  → Technically written-off accounts
  → Accounts under IBC proceedings (with NCLT's nod if required)

SETTLEMENT TERMS:
  → Amount: Board-approved; must follow policy
  → Minimum recovery: as per bank's approved policy
    (Typically: forced sale value of security at minimum)
  → Write-off: amount waived = write-off (provision used first, then P&L)

PROCESS:
  [ ] Borrower's OTS proposal received
  [ ] Valuation of security obtained (recent)
  [ ] NPA vintage considered (older NPA = more flexibility)
  [ ] Board/designated committee approval at bank
  [ ] OTS letter issued; settlement amount confirmed
  [ ] Payment received (lump sum or in agreed tranches)
  [ ] Account closed; NOC issued to borrower
  [ ] Documents returned; mortgage released

AUDIT CHECKS:
[ ] Board-approved OTS policy in place?
[ ] Each OTS: approved by authority as per DOA?
[ ] Recovery amount vs outstanding: haircut reasonable?
[ ] Not below forced sale value of security without higher authority?
[ ] No related party involvement (bank staff to borrower)
[ ] Tax implications: GST on OTS? TDS deducted?
[ ] Accounting: recovered amount vs provisions released vs write-off
[ ] Accounts receiving preferential treatment vs similarly placed NPAs?
[ ] Documentation: all required documents (NOC, discharge certificate) issued?

RED FLAGS IN OTS:
⚠  Settlement at far below security value without documented justification
⚠  OTS approved by authority below prescribed level
⚠  Borrower is related to bank official/board member
⚠  Settlement immediately after fresh valuation (inflated security value)
❌  No Board-approved policy for OTS
❌  OTS to wilful defaulters (prohibited per RBI circular)
```

## PART 7: DRT (DEBT RECOVERY TRIBUNAL) PROCEEDINGS

```
DRT PROCEEDINGS — AUDIT CHECKS:
[ ] Recovery certificate (RC) obtained from DRT? Date?
[ ] Execution of RC: recovery officer actions?
[ ] Amount recovered vs amount due: reconcile
[ ] DRT appeal by borrower (DRAT): stay on recovery? Impact on provision?
[ ] Time limits: DRT claim filed within 3 years of default (Limitation Act)
[ ] DRT claim amount: includes all dues (principal + interest + legal costs)?

RECOVERY PROCESS:
1. File claim with DRT (Recovery of Debts Due to Banks Act 1993)
2. DRT issues summons to borrower
3. Borrower files reply
4. Oral arguments
5. Judgment and Recovery Certificate (RC) issued
6. Recovery Officer executes RC (attachment, auction)

ACCOUNTING:
→ Any recovery received from DRT proceedings: first against provisions
→ If provisions fully absorbed: excess credit to P&L (income)
```

## PART 8: NARCL / NARCL-IDRCL AUDIT

```
NATIONAL ASSET RECONSTRUCTION COMPANY LIMITED (NARCL):
  Government-backed "Bad Bank" set up in 2021-22

NARCI PROCESS:
  → Banks transfer large NPAs to NARCL
  → NARCL pays: 15% cash + 85% Security Receipts (SRs)
  → SRs redeemable over time from recovery proceeds
  → IDRCL (India Debt Resolution Company Ltd) manages resolution

AUDITOR'S CHECKS FOR BANKS TRANSFERRING NPAs TO NARCL:
[ ] Transfer at Net Book Value (outstanding − provisions) or agreed price
[ ] SRs received: recognised at fair value on transfer date
[ ] 85% SR component: held as investment (category: AFS generally)
[ ] SR valuation: NAV-based; mark to market quarterly
[ ] 15% cash: upfront settlement (recognised in P&L vs provision)
[ ] Any recovery from NARCL distribution: credit to provisions/P&L
[ ] SRs exceeding 5 years: provisioned at progressive rates:
    Year 1-4: 0% provision on excess SRs above cash component
    Year 5: 50% provision on unrealised SR value
    Year 6+: 100% provision
[ ] NARCL transfer qualifies as de-recognition in books?
    → NPA removed from advances; SRs recognised in investments

KEY RBI CIRCULAR: RBI/2021-22/145 DOR.STR.REC.85/21.04.048/2021-22
```
