# SECTION A — STATUTORY CENTRAL AUDIT (SCA): COMPLETE 20-CHAPTER GUIDE
## ICAI Guidance Note on Audit of Banks (2025 Edition)

---

## OVERVIEW & LEGAL FRAMEWORK

The Statutory Central Auditor (SCA) conducts the apex-level audit of the ENTIRE bank.

```
LEGAL BASIS:
  Banking Regulation Act 1949 — Section 30
  Companies Act 2013 — Sections 139–148 + CARO 2020
  RBI Act 1934
  ICAI Standards on Auditing (SA 200–810)
  ICAI Guidance Note on Audit of Banks (2025 Edition)

SCA RESPONSIBILITIES:
  → Review all branch audit reports and MOCs
  → Issue consolidated audit report on bank's FS
  → Certify capital adequacy (CRAR — Basel III)
  → Issue CARO 2020 report (if applicable)
  → Consolidate and submit LFAR to Audit Committee of Board
  → Issue all regulatory certificates
  → Attend Audit Committee of Board (ACB)

BRANCH SELECTION FOR DETAILED AUDIT (SCA practice):
  → Top branches covering ~70% of advances (typically 10–15 branches)
  → All large NPA-heavy branches (mandatory full audit)
  → Treasury / forex branches (specialised)
  → Remaining branches: analytical review of reports + limited procedures
```

## CHAPTER 1: PERSONAL BANKING AUDIT

```
SCOPE: Retail deposits, personal lending, lockers, bancassurance

KEY AUDIT PROCEDURES:
[ ] Locker operations:
    - Physical inventory count on 31 March
    - Register of lockers: vacant vs occupied vs surrendered
    - Unclaimed lockers (5+ years): bank's surrender procedure followed?
    - Forced break-open register: RBI notification requirement
    - Locker rental income: correctly accrued (monthly)
    - Unoccupied lockers: rental waiver policy applied consistently?
[ ] Credit card portfolio (if at head office/zonal level):
    - Monthly billing: accuracy of interest and fees
    - Delinquency: 30/60/90/120 dpd buckets correctly classified?
    - NPA: >90 dpd → NPA with 100% provision requirement
    - Reward points liability: provisioned/accrued?
[ ] Bancassurance:
    - Trail income: correctly accrued over policy period
    - Upfront commission: amortised or expensed?
    - Insurance company agreements: within RBI bancassurance guidelines
    - Misselling risk: customer complaints relating to insurance
[ ] PMJDY (Jan Dhan Accounts):
    - Zero-balance: no charge levied per RBI directive
    - Overdraft facility: ₹10,000 limit (as revised); compliant?
    - RuPay card issuance: accounted?

RED FLAGS:
⚠  Locker register not reconciled for >1 year
❌  Insurance premium collected but not remitted to insurer
❌  Credit card NPA not classified (>90 dpd)
```

## CHAPTER 2: RETAIL BANKING AUDIT

```
SCOPE: Home loans, auto, personal, education, co-lending, PMAY, digital

EBLR COMPLIANCE (Post Oct 1, 2019 — MANDATORY):
[ ] ALL floating rate retail/MSME loans: linked to External Benchmark
    External Benchmarks: RBI Repo Rate / 3-month T-bill / 6-month T-bill
[ ] Reset frequency: at least once every 3 months
[ ] Spread over benchmark: CANNOT be increased post-sanction
    (Exception: credit risk of borrower demonstrably worsened)
[ ] Verify CBS: EBLR rate correctly updating on reset dates
[ ] Loans linked to MCLR (pre-Oct 2019): permissible; check reset date

HOME LOAN AUDIT:
[ ] LTV Compliance:
    Loan ≤₹30L:  max 90% LTV
    ₹30L-₹75L:   max 80% LTV
    >₹75L:        max 75% LTV
[ ] Stage disbursements: physical progress verification before each stage
[ ] PMAY subsidy (CLSS): claims submitted timely?
    Credit-linked subsidy: upfront interest subsidy
    NHB/HUDCO nodal agency verification

AUTO LOAN AUDIT:
[ ] RC book: hypothecation in bank's favour endorsed?
    (If not endorsed → flag in LFAR as documentation deficiency)
[ ] Comprehensive insurance: valid, bank as lossee
[ ] Repossession: legal repossession process per DRA Act
[ ] Post-repossession: auction/sale within 12 months; valuation obtained

CO-LENDING FRAMEWORK (RBI Nov 2020):
[ ] Co-lending agreement in place (bank + NBFC)
[ ] Bank's share ≥80%; NBFC's share ≥20%
[ ] Bank's credit underwriting standards applied (not just NBFC's)
[ ] KYC: bank-standard KYC (not lighter NBFC KYC)
[ ] Disbursement: to borrower directly (not through NBFC)
[ ] NBFC eligibility: positive net worth, CRAR ≥15%, no PCA
[ ] NPAs in co-lending portfolio: bank's share provisioned by bank
[ ] FLDG (First Loss Default Guarantee): ≤5% of loan portfolio

DIGITAL LENDING (RBI Sep 2022 Guidelines):
[ ] Only RBI-regulated entities as Lending Service Providers (LSPs)
[ ] Disbursement: directly to borrower's bank account
[ ] Repayment: directly to lender's bank account
[ ] KFS (Key Fact Statement): issued to each borrower pre-disbursement
[ ] APR (Annual Percentage Rate): prominently disclosed
[ ] Cooling-off period: 3 days for loans ≤₹50,000
[ ] No additional charges: only those disclosed in KFS
[ ] App permissions: only necessary permissions sought
[ ] Data localisation: borrower data in India
```

## CHAPTER 3: CORPORATE BANKING AUDIT

```
SCOPE: Large corporates, working capital, term loans, LEF, consortium, IBC

LARGE EXPOSURE FRAMEWORK (LEF) — RBI Master Circular:
[ ] Single Borrower/Counterparty limit: 25% of Eligible Capital Base (ECB)
    ECB = Tier 1 Capital
[ ] Group of Connected Counterparties: 35% of ECB
[ ] Check top 20 borrowers by exposure: within LEF?
[ ] Eligible exemptions: sovereign exposures, RBI, IFC-backed
[ ] Any breaches: remediation plan documented? RBI report filed?
[ ] Intra-group exposures: included in group LEF calculation

CONSORTIUM LENDING:
[ ] Lead bank: coordination responsibilities; JLF (if required)
[ ] ICA (Inter-Creditor Agreement): signed by all lenders?
[ ] Common data room / information sharing: functioning?
[ ] Escrow/dedicated account for cash flows
[ ] JLF (Joint Lenders' Forum): meetings held; minutes available?

IBC/NCLT ACCOUNTS (CRITICAL AREA):
STAGE 1 — CIRP ADMISSION:
[ ] Date of CIRP admission obtained from NCLT order
[ ] IRP (Interim Resolution Professional) appointed
[ ] Claims filing: bank's claim filed with IRP within 90 days of admission
[ ] Claim amount: principal + interest till CIRP admission date
[ ] Moratorium (Section 14 IBC): no recovery proceedings during CIRP

STAGE 2 — RESOLUTION PLAN:
[ ] RP (Resolution Professional) appointed after 30 days
[ ] RFRP (Request for Resolution Plans) issued?
[ ] Resolution plan received from applicants?
[ ] CoC (Committee of Creditors): bank's voting share = claim/total claims
[ ] Resolution plan approved: ≥66% vote of CoC (by value)
[ ] NCLT approval obtained for the resolution plan?
[ ] Haircut accepted: accounting treatment — write-off of haircut

STAGE 3 — LIQUIDATION:
[ ] CIRP period expired (180 days + 90 day extension)?
[ ] No resolution plan approved → liquidation ordered
[ ] Liquidation: 100% provision required IMMEDIATELY
[ ] Claims against liquidation estate: filed with liquidator?
[ ] Priority waterfall (Section 53 IBC):
    CIRP costs > Secured creditors > Unsecured creditors > Equity
[ ] Recovery from liquidation: credit to provisions

PROVISION TIMELINE UNDER IBC:
  On CIRP admission:              ≥50% provision (List 1)
  On admission (List 2 - large):  100% provision
  After 180 days without plan:    ≥50% if not already done
  After 270 days without plan:    Escalation; RBI-specific circular
  Liquidation ordered:            100% immediately
  Plan approved:                  Per haircut in plan

KEY RBI CIRCULAR: DBR.No.BP.BC.101/21.04.048/2017-18
```

## CHAPTER 4: PRIORITY SECTOR LENDING (PSL) — SCA LEVEL

```
CONSOLIDATED PSL COMPLIANCE:

TARGETS (Scheduled Commercial Banks — Domestic):
  Total PSL:                40% of ANBC or CEOBE (higher)
  Agriculture:              18% (of which 10% to SF/MF)
  Micro Enterprises:        7.5%
  Weaker Sections:          12%
  SFBs:                     75% PSL; 40% to micro enterprises

ANBC COMPUTATION (SCA — Consolidated):
  Net Bank Credit (NBC)
  + Outstanding FCNR(B) + NRNR Deposits
  + Eligible Co-lending outstandings
  − Lending to banks
  = ANBC

PSLC (Priority Sector Lending Certificates):
[ ] PSLCs purchased: credited to buyer's PSL target
[ ] PSLCs sold: deducted from seller's PSL
[ ] Four types: PSLC-Agriculture, PSLC-SF/MF, PSLC-Micro, PSLC-General
[ ] All PSLC trades on RBI-designated e-Kuber platform
[ ] Verify trades and balance as at 31 March

SHORTFALL CONSEQUENCES:
  PSL shortfall → Contribution to:
  RIDF (Rural Infrastructure Development Fund) at NABARD
  SIDBI Fund / NHB Fund / MUDRA Ltd
  Interest on RIDF < market rate (disincentive)
[ ] Verify RIDF/SIDBI/NHB/MUDRA contributions match PSL shortfall
[ ] PSL certificate: unqualified if targets met; qualified if shortfall
```

## CHAPTER 5: TREASURY OPERATIONS AUDIT

```
INVESTMENT PORTFOLIO — COMPREHENSIVE:

CLASSIFICATION RULES (RBI Master Circular on Investments):
HTM (Held to Maturity):
  → Board-approved list of securities
  → Proportion: max 25% of investments (with RBI approval up to SLR)
  → Accounting: amortised cost; no MTM
  → Annual transfer window (April 1): shifts allowed once; excess depreciation to P&L
  → No deliberate sale from HTM (except exigencies — RBI circular)

AFS (Available for Sale):
  → All other investments not classified HTM/HFT
  → Quarterly MTM: lower of book value or market value (scrip-wise)
  → Depreciation: charged to P&L immediately
  → Appreciation: NOT recognised in P&L
  → Investment Fluctuation Reserve (IFR): target 5% of AFS+HFT
    - Depreciation first charged to IFR; excess to P&L
    - Appreciation first credited to IFR; excess after restoring to P&L

HFT (Held for Trading):
  → Daily MTM: MTM P&L realised immediately
  → Intra-day trading desk positions
  → 90-day holding period rule (if not sold within 90 days → transfer to AFS)

VALUATION SOURCES (as at 31 March):
  G-Secs: FIMMDA/FBIL rates
  CDs/CPs: FIMMDA/FBIL
  Corporate Bonds: FIMMDA matrix (for illiquid bonds)
  Equity: BSE/NSE closing price
  MFs: NAV as published on 31 March

NPI (Non-Performing Investments):
[ ] Identify all debt securities where interest/principal overdue >90 days
[ ] Sub-Standard NPI: 10% provision
[ ] Doubtful NPI (12-24m): 20% provision
[ ] Doubtful NPI (24-36m): 30% provision
[ ] Doubtful NPI (>36m): 50% provision
[ ] Loss NPI: 100% provision

DERIVATIVES AUDIT:
[ ] IRS (Interest Rate Swaps): fair value on 31 March per ISDA methodology
[ ] FRA (Forward Rate Agreements): fair value + accruals
[ ] Options: intrinsic + time value; separately disclosed
[ ] Counterparty limits: PFE (Potential Future Exposure) within Board limits
[ ] CCIL Clearing: all reportable OTC derivatives centrally cleared?
[ ] MTM margin calls: promptly met?
[ ] Collateral pledged: disclosed in notes?

ALM (Asset-Liability Management):
[ ] ALM Policy: Board-approved; current?
[ ] 1-14 day liquidity bucket: gap ≤20% of outflows (RBI requirement)
[ ] 1-90 day cumulative gap: within policy limits
[ ] Earning at Risk (EaR): disclosed?
[ ] LCR: ≥100%; verify computation
    LCR = HQLA Stock / Net Cash Outflows (30-day stress scenario)
[ ] NSFR: ≥100%
    NSFR = Available Stable Funding / Required Stable Funding
```

## CHAPTER 7: AGRICULTURAL BANKING AUDIT

```
AGRICULTURAL NPA — SPECIAL RULES (Critical):

SHORT DURATION CROPS (paddy, wheat, jowar, maize etc. — one season):
  NPA if not repaid within 2 crop seasons from DUE DATE
  (Not from disbursement — from the scheduled repayment date)
  Example: Kharif crop loan due by Jan 31
  → NPA if unpaid by Jan 31 of NEXT YEAR (2 seasons = ~12 months)

LONG DURATION CROPS (sugarcane, plantation, tea, coffee, rubber):
  NPA if not repaid within 1 crop season from due date
  Typical crop season: 1 year for plantation crops

ALLIED ACTIVITIES (dairy, poultry, fisheries, animal husbandry, apiculture):
  NORMAL 90-day NPA rule applies (same as term loans)

KCC (KISAN CREDIT CARD) AUDIT:
[ ] Scale of Finance: fixed by DLTC/SLTC; bank using correct SoF?
[ ] Limit calculation: 1 year crop expense + post-harvest + maintenance
    + consumption + allied activities (if combined KCC)
[ ] Land records: 7/12 extract or equivalent verified at sanction
[ ] Annual limit review: done and recorded?
[ ] KCC NPA: seasonal repayment; track by crop season, not calendar month
[ ] PM-KISAN linkage: ₹6,000 annual subsidy credited to KCC?

INTEREST SUBVENTION SCHEME (Kisan Credit Cards):
  Short-term crop loans ≤₹3 Lakhs at 7% p.a. to farmers
  Government provides:
    → 1.5% interest subvention to banks (to make effective rate 7%)
    → 3% prompt repayment incentive (if paid within due date — farmer gets 4% p.a.)
  
[ ] Verify claims submitted to NABARD/Government correctly
[ ] Ensure subvention income NOT inflated in P&L
[ ] Prompt repayment incentive: verify disbursement to eligible farmers

NATURAL CALAMITY / DROUGHT RELIEF:
[ ] State/Central government declaration of calamity: obtained?
[ ] RBI circular allowing rescheduling without NPA classification?
[ ] Rescheduled accounts: not classified NPA but monitored
[ ] NABARD/Government compensation to banks: correctly accounted

NABARD REFINANCE:
[ ] Short-term refinance (STCRC): crop loan refinance
[ ] Long-term refinance: agri infrastructure projects
[ ] End-use verification: refinanced funds used for stated purpose?
[ ] NABARD inspection findings: complied?
```

## CHAPTER 8: TRADE FINANCE AUDIT

```
LETTERS OF CREDIT (LC):

TYPES:
  Sight LC (SBLC): payable on presentation of documents
  Usance LC: payable after fixed days (60/90/120/180 days)
  Revolving LC: auto-renewing limit
  Back-to-Back LC: second LC issued against first LC
  Standby LC: used as payment guarantee

AUDIT CHECKS:
[ ] LC limits within sanctioned sub-limits (sanction letter)
[ ] LC documentation: compliant with UCP 600 (ICC rules)
[ ] Document discrepancies: bank's acceptance of discrepant docs → risk
[ ] Devolved LCs (importer defaulted on payment):
    - Devolvement creates a LOAN to the LC applicant
    - If overdue >90 days → classify as NPA
    - Many hidden NPAs discovered here — check CBS carefully
[ ] Import LC aging: all outstanding LCs tracked; overdue LCs identified
[ ] Nostro payment: correspondent bank paid on time?

BANK GUARANTEES (BG):

TYPES:
  Performance Guarantee (PG): for contract performance
  Financial Guarantee (FG): for financial obligations
  Bid Bond/Tender Guarantee: during tender process
  Advance Payment Guarantee (APG): for advance repayment

AUDIT CHECKS:
[ ] BG register: complete, with all active guarantees listed
[ ] Maturity tracking: expiry tracked; extension letters collected
[ ] Invoked BGs:
    - Was invocation legal and within terms?
    - Recovery from borrower initiated immediately?
    - If overdue >90 days from invocation → NPA!
[ ] BG commission income: amortised over tenure; NOT upfront recognition
[ ] Contingent liability disclosure: all outstanding BGs in Notes to Accounts

EXPORT FINANCE:
[ ] PCFC (Pre-Shipment Credit in Foreign Currency):
    - Rate: not to exceed SOFR (formerly LIBOR) + 200 bps
    - Period: up to 180 days (360 days for project/capital goods export)
[ ] EPC (Export Packing Credit): rupee pre-shipment credit
[ ] Post-shipment credit (PSCFC/PSFC): against export bills
[ ] ECGC Cover: policy valid; bank as beneficiary
[ ] Export bills outstanding > 6 months: RBI/FEMA compliance
    - Report in EDPMS (Export Data Processing and Monitoring System)
    - Bank's responsibility to report and pursue recovery
```

## CHAPTER 13: RISK MANAGEMENT

```
CREDIT RISK:
[ ] Credit Rating Migration: % of accounts downgraded vs upgraded YoY
[ ] Sector Concentration: top 3 sectors as % of advances
    (Real estate, infra, textiles, gems/jewellery = traditionally risky)
[ ] Single party concentration: LEF compliance (covered in Ch. 3)
[ ] Forward-looking provisioning: Expected Credit Loss (ECL) under Ind AS 109
    → Stage 1: Performing — 12-month ECL
    → Stage 2: Significant credit deterioration — Lifetime ECL
    → Stage 3: Credit-impaired (NPA equivalent) — Lifetime ECL
    → Indian banks transitioning to Ind AS 109; some may still use IGAAP

MARKET RISK:
[ ] VaR (Value at Risk): Board-approved limits for trading book
    - 99% confidence interval, 10-day holding period (Basel requirement)
[ ] Stress testing: parallel shifts, twist, credit spread shocks
[ ] Back-testing: P&L vs VaR — backtesting exceptions reported?
[ ] Sensitivity analysis: DV01 (Dollar Value of 01 bp change) limits

OPERATIONAL RISK:
[ ] BIA (Basic Indicator Approach): Capital = 15% × Average Gross Income (3 yrs)
[ ] Gross Income computation: correct?
[ ] Operational risk events: collected in loss database (internal)
[ ] External loss data: ORIC-India database subscribed?

LIQUIDITY RISK:
[ ] LCR: ≥100% daily; HQLA buffer adequate
[ ] NSFR: ≥100%
[ ] Intraday liquidity: peak position tracked
[ ] Liquidity stress testing: scenarios run; results to Board/ALCO

ICAAP (Internal Capital Adequacy Assessment Process):
[ ] Covers all Pillar 2 risks: credit concentration, interest rate (banking book), liquidity, reputation
[ ] Capital planning horizon: 3 years
[ ] Stress scenarios: severe but plausible
[ ] Board-approved; submitted to RBI as part of SREP dialogue
[ ] RBI's Supervisory Review (SREP): Pillar 2 capital add-on if any
```

```

## CHAPTER 15: CREDIT MONITORING & RESTRUCTURING

```
CREDIT MONITORING (SCA LEVEL):
[ ] Early Warning System (EWS): RBI-mandated; bank has it in place?
    Triggers: Financial distress signals, account conduct, market intelligence
[ ] CRILC data accuracy: all ≥₹5Cr exposure reported correctly
